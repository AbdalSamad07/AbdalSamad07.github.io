Bootcamp 2020: Retro 2 Player Game

This is my first project carried out to conclude the 2 weeks of Bootcamp that took place at City. This processing project demonstrates various uses of technique such as classes and objects. Also, shows many use of condition statements to carry out certain actions within the game. I created and developed a Retro Ball Game that allows 2 player functionality. To layout the structure of the coding, I used classes such as Ball and Player to distinguish the main functions of the game. Each player is controlled using either "w" and "s" or "UP and "DOWN". The main aimof this game is to guide the players position to score in the opposing goal. However, players can only move vertically across the game's setting. After every goal scored, the ball will reposition itself in the centre and will shoot out to the opposing player. Once one of the player reaches 10 points, the game is finished.
